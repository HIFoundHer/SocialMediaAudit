The Core Shift

Stop posting about FoundHer.
Start posting for her.

Right now the account posts about FoundHer — its events, its cohort, its program. What it needs to become is a resource account that posts for the woman who should be a FoundHer — one she'd follow even if she never applied, because it makes her business life better every single week.

The Instagram benchmark for engagement is 2–5%. FoundHer's audience is averaging 7–11%. That means the people who follow you care deeply. The problem isn't retention — it's that the experience isn't compelling enough to convert curious visitors into followers, and it isn't consistent enough to build the habit of showing up.

Now: "Here's what FoundHer did this month."

Future: "Here's what every Hawaiʻi wāhine founder needs to know this week."

Visual Diagnosis

The grid reads as five different accounts.

Different fonts. Different color palettes. Different graphic styles. Different photo treatments. No consistent brand architecture. A visitor landing on the profile has no visual cue that says this is a brand worth trusting.

The butterfly logo is lovely and almost entirely absent from content. It should be a consistent watermark and framing device. The mission is extraordinary — the visual system doesn't yet match it.

No cohesive palette Inconsistent fonts Collab posts cluttering grid No Reels strategy Bio speaks to applicants not followers No CTA architecture
Going Forward — Visual Identity System

The look we're building toward.

Montserrat will be our type family across all content. Headlines in Montserrat Black, supporting text in Regular or Bold, and all body copy in Extra Light. The icon logo only on images — the full wordmark fights for visual dominance with headlines. And a brand color overlay on most images to keep the feed cohesive at a glance.

What's not working
1
"Accelerating founders" is applicant language
It describes the program's function, not the person reading it. A curious first-time visitor who isn't ready to apply doesn't see herself in "accelerating."
2
"Program · Community · Funding" is a spec sheet
Features, not a promise. It tells you what exists, not what changes for you. No aspiration, no transformation — just a product description.
3
"Meet our 2026 cohort" closes the door on everyone else
This CTA only makes sense if you're actively applying. It gives non-applicants — the majority of profile visitors — zero reason to follow or stay.

A visitor should feel seen within the first line — "that's me." Right now the bio describes who FoundHer serves in the third person rather than speaking directly to her.

Our Audience
"She doesn't think she's ready.
She is. She's exactly who we're talking to."

She's Native Hawaiian, Pacific Islander, or Asian American. Running her business for 1–3 years, making $25K–$120K. Deeply rooted in culture and community. She has never been handed a roadmap for what "scaling" looks like without selling her soul or leaving her kids behind.

She scrolls Instagram during the rare 20 minutes she has to herself, looking for something that feels like it was made for her — not for a generic hustle-culture founder bro in Silicon Valley.

  • 💸

  • 👶
    She feels like she has to choose between her business and her family — the ʻohana care stipend story speaks directly to her
  • 📊
    She's surrounded by business advice that wasn't built for women or for Hawaiʻi — our curriculum content fills this gap
  • 🤔
    She second-guesses whether her "small" business deserves serious support — our founder stories prove she belongs
  • 🌺
    She wants to grow without compromising her values or her culture — the Wāhine Way content validates her whole approach
The Content Test
"Does this make her feel seen, smarter, or more equipped?"
Every post, every caption, every video should pass this test before it goes live. If the answer is no — if it's primarily serving FoundHer's promotional needs rather than her actual needs — reconsider whether it belongs on the feed.

Video Strategy

Every video needs a purpose architecture.

Current videos are warm introductions. A viewer finishes and doesn't know what they learned, what to do next, or why it mattered. Here's the framework that fixes that.

1
Hook
First 1–3 seconds. On-screen text AND spoken. State the most interesting or surprising thing about this video. Not "Hi I'm Skye" — something that creates immediate tension.
"I turned a $20K grant into a $200K business in 18 months. Here's how."
2
The Point
One specific insight, story, or lesson. Not a general about me. Not a program overview. Something the viewer can use or be genuinely moved by.
The one decision, the one turning point, the one thing she did differently.
3
Callback
Near the end, return to the hook. Resolve the tension you created in second one. Give the viewer the payoff they stayed for.
"Remember that $20K? Here's exactly where it went — and what it became."
4
CTA
Last 5 seconds. Explicit. One action only. Follow. Apply. Save. Share. Never leave a viewer without somewhere to go next.
"Applications open now. Link in bio. You're more ready than you think."

Collab Post Policy

Clean up the grid. Own the visual.

Collaboration feed posts from other accounts clutter the grid and break visual cohesion. Going forward, FoundHer controls the visual — always.

🚫
Archive existing collab posts that break visual cohesion
Go through the current feed and archive any collaboration posts that don't fit the new visual system. This doesn't delete them — they can be restored — but they clear the grid for a fresh brand experience.
✓
FoundHer creates the post, tags partners as collaborators
When we collab on events or content, FoundHer makes the post — we control the visual, the copy, the template. Partners get tagged as Instagram collaborators (they appear in their feed too), but our grid stays cohesive.
✓
Reshare to Stories only — never as permanent feed posts
When other FoundHer companies or partners post content that mentions or features FoundHer, share it to our Stories. Celebrate them loudly there. The feed is for our brand system. Stories are for community amplification.
✓
Tag all FoundHer companies on event posts — always
This is non-negotiable for events. Each company tagged = their followers see the post. 12 companies tagged = 12 audiences reached. This is the simplest and most powerful reach multiplier available to us right now.
